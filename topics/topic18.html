Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)

SDLC is a step-by-step process followed by software teams to design, develop, test, and maintain software with high quality and low cost.

Phases of SDLC

1. Requirement Gathering – understand client needs. 2. Planning – estimate cost, time, and resources. 3. Design – create architecture and UI design. 4. Development – programmers write code. 5. Testing – check bugs and errors. 6. Deployment – release software to users. 7. Maintenance – updates and improvements.

Popular SDLC Models

- Waterfall - Agile - Spiral - V-Model - DevOps

Why SDLC Is Important

SDLC gives structure to software projects and ensures quality, timelines, and efficiency. Every IT company uses SDLC in real projects.
